The Problem

Why Generic Voice-to-Text Fails
UK Accountants

Consumer dictation tools were built for personal use — not for VAT returns, corporation tax computations, or HMRC correspondence.

🧾

They mangle accounting terminology

Generic tools transcribe "PAYE settlement agreement" as gibberish, get confused by HMRC form numbers, and mis-hear financial figures — forcing tedious manual correction on every document.

🔒

They upload client data to the cloud

Client financial information is highly sensitive. Tools that stream every spoken word to a corporate server create potential GDPR breaches and expose you to professional body sanctions under ICAEW, ACCA, or CIMA guidelines.

⏱️

They don't track billable time

UK accountancy practices lose thousands of pounds of billable work annually because time spent on dictation and correspondence goes unlogged. Without integrated time capture, you are permanently under-billing.

📋

They produce unformatted output

Engagement letters, management account narratives, and tax computation notes all follow structured conventions. Dumping raw dictation text means manual reformatting — erasing any time saving you gained.
