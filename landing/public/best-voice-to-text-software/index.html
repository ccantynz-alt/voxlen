The Problem

Why Most Voice to Text Tools
Disappoint Professionals

The consumer market is flooded with voice tools that work for grocery lists — but fall apart when you need to draft contracts, financial reports, or client correspondence.

📉

Accuracy isn't good enough

Consumer tools average 85–92% word accuracy. At 500 words per minute, that's 40–75 errors per minute — more work correcting than it saves. Professionals need 99%+ accuracy to benefit from dictation.

💻

Platform gaps leave you stranded

Dragon is Windows-only. Apple Dictation doesn't work on Windows. Whispr Flow is Mac-only. Most web-based tools don't have a universal hotkey. Professionals who switch between devices need one consistent tool.

☁️

Everything goes to the cloud

Otter.ai, Google Voice, and most subscription tools upload every word you say to their servers. For lawyers, accountants, or executives working with confidential information, this creates unacceptable data risk.

✏️

Raw transcription still needs editing

None of the leading tools apply intelligent grammar correction by default. You get a wall of run-on sentences that still need heavy manual editing — the very thing dictation is supposed to eliminate.
