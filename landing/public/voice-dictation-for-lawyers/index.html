The Problem

Why Generic Dictation Tools
Fail Lawyers

General-purpose voice-to-text was built for emails and shopping lists — not for law practice, where every word has legal consequence and every minute is potentially billable.

⚖️

They mangle legal terminology

Generic tools transcribe res ipsa loquitur as gibberish and butcher Latin maxims, case citations, and procedural terms — forcing manual correction after every dictation session.

🔒

They upload privileged content to servers

Attorney-client privilege extends to how you store and transmit client information. Tools that upload every word to a corporate server create an ethics risk most bar associations haven't explicitly cleared.

📄

They produce unformatted walls of text

Court filings, depositions, and contracts follow rigid formatting conventions. Generic dictation dumps raw text and leaves all formatting to you — defeating the purpose of dictation.

🕐

They ignore billable time

Every minute of dictation is potentially billable. Without integrated time tracking, lawyers leave revenue on the table or waste time manually logging hours after the fact.
