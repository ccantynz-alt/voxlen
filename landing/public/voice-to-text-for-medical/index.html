The Problem

Why Generic Dictation Tools
Fail Healthcare Professionals

Consumer voice-to-text was designed for sending messages — not for clinical documentation, discharge summaries, or referral letters.

🩺

They mis-transcribe clinical terms

Generic tools regularly mis-hear drug names, anatomical terms, and diagnostic language — turning "metformin 500mg" into something unrecognisable and creating dangerous clinical documentation errors that require tedious manual correction.

🔒

They upload patient data to the cloud

Most consumer dictation tools stream audio to remote servers in real time. In healthcare, every spoken word during a patient encounter may constitute Protected Health Information. Sending PHI to a consumer cloud service creates HIPAA, GDPR, and NHS DSP Toolkit compliance risks.

📋

They produce unstructured output

Clinical documentation follows strict formats — SOAP notes, discharge summaries, referral letters, operative reports. Consumer tools dump raw dictation as an unformatted wall of text, leaving all structuring work to the clinician.

💰

Specialist medical tools cost a fortune

Dragon Medical One charges $99/month or more per user. For a small practice or a consultant working independently, that cost is hard to justify — especially when the software requires significant setup time and IT support.
