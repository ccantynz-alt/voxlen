The Problem

Why Generic Dictation Tools
Fail Lawyers

General-purpose voice-to-text was designed for emails and shopping lists — not for drafting motions, depositions, or demand letters.

⚖️

They mangle legal terminology

Generic tools transcribe "res ipsa loquitur" as gibberish and butcher Latin maxims, case citations, and procedural terms — forcing manual correction after every dictation.

🔒

They send your content to the cloud

Attorney-client privilege extends to how you store and transmit client information. Tools that upload every word to a corporate server create an ethics risk most bar associations haven't explicitly cleared.

📄

They produce unformatted walls of text

Court filings, depositions, and contracts all follow rigid formatting conventions. Generic dictation tools dump raw text and leave formatting entirely to you.

🕐

They don't track billable time

Every minute of dictation is potentially billable. Without integrated time tracking, lawyers leave revenue on the table or waste time manually logging hours after the fact.
