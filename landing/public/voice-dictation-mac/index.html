FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Voxlen for Mac.

Is Voxlen a good Dragon NaturallySpeaking alternative for Mac?
Yes. Dragon NaturallySpeaking (Dragon Professional) was discontinued on macOS in 2023, leaving Mac users without enterprise-grade dictation software. Voxlen fills that gap with modern AI — faster, more accurate, natively built for macOS including Apple Silicon, and actively maintained.
Does Voxlen work on Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, M4)?
Yes. Voxlen ships as a universal binary and runs natively on both Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, M4 and later) and Intel Macs. You get the full performance of your chip with no Rosetta translation needed on any Mac.
Which Mac apps does Voxlen work in?
Voxlen works in virtually any Mac application — Microsoft Word, Outlook, Apple Mail, Safari, Chrome, Firefox, Notion, Google Docs, legal practice management software, accounting platforms, and more. It injects text directly at the OS level using macOS Accessibility APIs, so wherever you can type, Voxlen can dictate.
Is my dictation data private?
Voxlen is designed for professionals such as lawyers and accountants who require strict confidentiality. All vocabulary learning and usage patterns are stored on-device only — your Mac, never our servers. Voxlen never stores your audio or transcripts — requests pass through zero-retention endpoints straight to the AI providers, and nothing is retained.
What macOS versions does Voxlen support?
Voxlen supports macOS 12 Monterey and later, including macOS 13 Ventura, macOS 14 Sonoma, and macOS 15 Sequoia. We test on the latest major macOS release with every update.
